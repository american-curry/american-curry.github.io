A perfect 4 day budget-friendly guide to New York City

As has already been said so many times, it is impossible to see New York in one visit. So this blog covers all the things that we saw during our trip to New York as well as other potential destinations that you can visit if you have more time.

As an international student visiting NYC for the first time, our primary focus was: Budget, Food+Drinks, Landmarks, and Fun. The itinerary below had us on our feet hurting while we enjoyed our visit! A number of itineraries found online can be either sponsored (suggesting buying tours, for example) and are too tight, so we decided to write up an easy-to-follow itinerary.

Things to do before the trip:

Book flight/train tickets

Flights can get quite costly near your trip date. Plan well ahead.

Make a hotel reservation

Good Budget Hotels are expected to fill up pretty fast near a holiday. Ensure to book travel and hotels together.

Buy the New York city pass and reserve times for when you want to visit these destinations (it's always a good idea to make reservations asap, especially if you're visiting during peak periods)

City pass is a good saving tip for visiting NYC. It covers a number of first-timer destinations. Note that it is important to reserve times to the destinations after you buy your city-pass. The timings can be taken from the itinerary below as we relied on the city pass for our trip.

Make reservations for whichever restaurants and speakeasies you can (this is especially important if you're visiting on weekends)

You dont need to worry about reservations in Hole-in-the-wall and street food locations but for speakeasies/restaurants and pubs its a good idea to check if they require reservations beforehand.

Day 1 - Evening

Grand Central Terminal - MetroCard

Day 1 evening starts at the Grand Central Terminal, where you can get the metro pass. This pass can be used on the subways as well as the buses, and you will use this to get everywhere in the city. Also, the subway network in NY has a life of its own that you don't want to miss.

Even if you are not going to Grand Central Terminal to get your metro pass as I did, I recommend going there as it is a charming station where so many movies have been shot. This is also where you can start your NY food journey with Magnolia bakery. They are famous for their Banana Pudding (which is other-worldly), but their cupcakes and cakes are no joke either.

Speakeasy - Bathtub Gin

Head directly to one of the speakeasies/rooftop bars in the city (our recommendation - Bathtub Gin) next, which has some great cocktails and decent food.

Times Square

The next and last stop for the night was Times Square, which was full of tourists at this hour but the vibe was amazing. I would recommend coming here at night as you can see all the billboards lit up. Also, you cannot click a bad picture here, literally. You'll find plenty of good and affordable souvenir shops on these streets and some pretty good dupes of high-end bags as well. But please be mindful of your bags/belongings here.

Day 2 - Morning

9/11 Memorial

Start the morning somber with a visit to the 9/11 memorial museum. You might feel like skipping this, but trust me, you'll be missing out. I never realized how little I knew of the attack before coming here, and listening to first-hand accounts from the survivors of the attack will make you feel fortunate as well as humbled. Not to mention, the memorial fountains right outside the museum are beautiful.

Oculus

The next stop will be the Oculus, which is right next to the museum. There's not much to say about it except that it looks like the skeleton of a dinosaur and is basically a shopping mall on the inside, but it is charming to look at, probably an excellent example of contemporary architecture. There's also some pretty cool graffiti outside the Oculus that will give you a feel of the New York culture.

Lunch: Los Tacos No. 1

You can lunch in Los Tacos No. 1, which truly is No. 1 for Mexican food in NY. They also have some great vegetarian options, and don't sleep on the beverages they have to offer. Just be careful with the hot sauce xD.

Day 2 - Afternoon

Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island

The next stop is the Statue of Liberty (yay!). You will go to Battery Park to catch the ferry to the Liberty Island. While on the ferry, I would recommend going to the top deck as this is where you will get a magnificent view of the city as well as the Statue of Liberty. Please note that you'll probably need to make a prior reservation for pedestal and crown access (that's right, you can stand in the crown of Lady Liberty!).

The next stop on the ferry is Ellis Island, which houses the National Museum of Immigration, where you can educate yourself on what the refugees first saw/experiences where they arrived in America.

Ferry's run at frequent intervals between Statue-Ellis-Battery which makes it very convenient. Aaaand then you're back to Battery Park.

You could also visit wall street from here and click a picture with the famous bull statue.

Day 2 - Evening

Brooklyn Bridge

For a perfect end to the day, wear your most comfy pair of shoes and walk across the Brooklyn Bridge as the sun goes down. Another excellent place for a gorgeous view of the city and some amazing clicks.

Dinner - Neapolitan Pizza in NYC

For dinner, if you were able to make a reservation for Lucali's, I'm low-key jealous of you. If not, worry not! You can go to Keste Pizza and Vino, which is less crowded, has amazing pizza and pasta and great wine to go with it. I recommend everything about this restaurant except its decor, which is either vintage or just old.

Day 3 - Morning

This happened to be the 4th of July for us, so plans were based mostly on whatever was open.

Breakfast - Bagel

Start the day like a true New Yorker - with a bagel. Ess-a-bagel/Absolute Bagels are the best places for this, but be prepared to wait in the line.

Top of the Rock

Next stop is the Top of the Rock. If you walk there, you'll be blessed with some pretty charming views of the city. While the most stunning view of the city will be at night, I recommend trying one of the high spots (Top of the Rock, the Edge, the Empire State Building) during the day just to get an idea of the layout of the city and be overwhelmed by it. Top of the Rock is a good choice for it.

If you have some time to spend here, you can also go shopping at the Rockefeller plaza.

Day 3 - Afternoon

Again, wear your most comfy shoes because you're going to walk through a plenty art galleries today.

Metropolitan Museum of Art - The Met

You can go to the Metropolitan Museum of Art next. This might be an unpopular opinion, but if you have limited time and have been to plenty of museums before, you can skip this. Call me uncultured, but I would rather have absorbed more of the city than visited the MET.

If you do go to the museum, here's what you should not sleep on - Greek and Roman Sculpture Court (Gallery 162), Van Gogh and Monet paintings (Gallery 826), The Temple of Dendur (Gallery 131), The American Wing Court (Gallery 700).

You'll also enjoy the vibe outside of the museum, with all the food stalls and street performances.

Next, you can visit the American Museum of Natural History, which is not too far and was where Night at the Museum movie was shot.

Lunch

I lunched at the Shake Shack - which I do not recommend. Might just be my experience, but they had the worst fries and vegetarian burger I ever had.

Don't miss out on the Levain Bakery though and try their Chocolate Chip Walnut cookies.

Day 3 - Evening

4th of July Celebrations üéâ

As I mentioned before, Day 3, for us was the 4th of July. So, the evening involved rushing to the 42nd, then 46th, and then 48th St. trying to find a spot to look at the Macy's fireworks. If you're in New York on the 4th of July and you're not watching the fireworks, you're wasting your trip, no joke.

If you have some extra cash to spend, you can also go to any of the many cruises and view the fireworks from the East River.

Day 4 - Morning

Central Park

Missing out on Central Park is definitely a bad idea on your trip to NYC. Central Park is a serene place of lush greenery transporting you out of the concrete jungle situated right in the heart of Manhattan. Start your morning there possibly with a food takeout (Central Park is huge, you'll probably get hungry). In this visit to Central Park, visit the following locations:

  • Belvedere castle
  • Bethesda Fountain and Terrace
  • Strawberry Fields (a memorial to John Lennon)

Day 4 - Afternoon

Beautiful New York neighborhoods

After a nice relaxing morning at Central Park, head out towards the scenic/foodie neighborhoods of NYC. Some options are Little Italy and Chinatown, but on our trip, we headed straight to Washington Square Park to get a bite of the best NY dosas but were disappointed with the long queue with ETA of 2 hours. In the afternoon, roam around the lively neighborhood of Greenwich village starting from Washington Square Park. Grab a falafel at Mamoun's (best falafel in NYC) and a Cronut at Dominque Ansel Bakery on our foot tour.

Chelsea Market and the High Line park

Take a subway to Chelsea Market - a beautiful indoor shopping + food destination situation right in front of Google's NYC office. Avoid overpriced foods and skip unnecessary lines for food joints which you can get elsewhere (like Los Tacos No. 1).

A short walk from Chelsea market is the High Line Park - a 1.45-mile park built on an old metro line passing right through the city. Enjoy a nice walk and great views of the city. Expect the park to be crowded as the walking area is quite narrow.

Take some rest and food at your hotel before heading out to another speakeasy! We ordered in some tasty Indian Food from Dhaba Restaurant (their Vada Pav's were amazing!).

Day 4 - Evening

Drinks at a speakeasy

speakeasy: (during Prohibition) an illicit liquor store or nightclub.

Speakeasies are hidden/secret locations that serve drinks and unique cocktails with beautiful interiors. A good option is to head out to The Garret which has a young vibe and is a small but cozy location serving a variety of amazing cocktails. The entrance to the bar is from the inside of Five Guy, which will give make you feel like you sneaking in to get liquor during prohibition! Note that in our visit there were no food options here, so eating beforehand is recommended.

Empire State Building

Make a late-night reservation to the Empire state building, in our case we made the reservation for 11 PM. After seeing NYC from the top of the rock, it cannot be stressed enough that you choose the second observation spot visit to be at night. The city looks completely different and the view at night will leave you in awe. Also, you can expect fewer visitors which can help you in taking your pictures unhindered. The cool breeze at night on the 86th floor of the Empire State Building with the view as shown below will be a perfect way to cap off your day.

(Optionally) Head out to Times Square.

Day 5 - Morning

If you ever find yourself with some extra time in New York (how?!), go to Central Park! I went to the zoo, which is like most other zoos, but in Central Park! Top attractions will be the Red Panda, snow leopard, the Grizzley Bear, and the aviary. You can also visit the petting zoo here (which is in the Tisch Children's Zoo), but they don't have any dogs if that's why you're going (smh in self-disappointment).
