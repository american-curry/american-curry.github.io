New York Details

Day 1 - evening

Day 1 evening starts at the Grand Central Terminal, where you can get the metro pass. This pass can be used on the subways as well as the buses, and you will use this to get everywhere in the city. Also, the subway network in NY has a life of its own that you don't want to miss.

Even if you are not going to Grand Central Terminal to get your metro pass as I did, I recommend going there as it is a charming station where so many movies have been shot. This is also where you can start your NY food journey with Magnolia bakery. They are famous for their Banana Pudding (which is other-worldly), but their cupcakes and cakes are no joke either.

We went to the Bathtub Gin next, which has some great cocktails and pretty good food (you have to try the fries here). You might not be able to get a lot of good pictures here, if that is the focus of your trip, but I can promise that you'll love their vibe! They also have an old-timey toilet which is kind of an attraction (or is it?!).

The next and last stop for the night was Times Square, which was full of tourists at this hour. I would recommend coming here at night as you can see all the billboards lit up. Also, you cannot click a bad picture here, literally. You'll find plenty of good and affordable souvenir shops on these streets and some pretty good dupes of high-end brand bags as well. But please be mindful of your bags/belongings here.

Day 2 - morning

Start the morning somber with a visit to the 9/11 memorial museum. You might feel like skipping this, but trust me, you'll be missing out. I never realized how little I knew of the attack before coming here and listening to first hand accounts from the survivors of the attack will make you feel fortunate as well as humbled. Not to mention, the memorial fountains right outside the museum are beautiful.

The next stop will be the Oculus, which is right next to the museum. There's not much to say about it except that it looks like the skeleton of a (maybe deformed) dinosaur and is basically a shopping mall on the inside, but it is charming to look at, probably an excellent example of contemporary architecture. There's also some pretty cool wall art outside the Oculus which is very cool to look at and will give you a feel of the New York culture.

You can lunch in Los Tacos No. 1, which truly is No. 1 for Mexican food in NY. They also have some great vegetarian options and don't sleep on the beverages they have to offer. Just be careful with the hot sauce XD.

Day 2 - afternoon

The next stop is the Statue of Liberty (yay!). You will go to Battery Park (also very beautiful) to catch the ferry to the Liberty Island. While on the ferry, I would recommend going to the top deck as this is where you will get a magnificent view of the city as well as the Statue of Liberty (how the first refugees saw it? please ignore my ignorance). The island is gorgeous. The Statue is gorgeous. The view of the city is gorgeous. Please note that you'll probably need to make a prior reservation for pedestal and crown access (that's right, you can stand in the crown of Lady Liberty).

The next stop on the ferry is Ellis Island, which houses the National Museum of Immigration, where you can educate yourself on what the refugees first saw/experiences where they arrived in America.

Aaaand you're back to Battery Park.

You could also visit wall street from here and click a picture with the bull (I hear rubbing its balls gives you good luck for a year).

Day 2 - evening

For a perfect end to the day, wear your most comfy pair of shoes and walk across the Brooklyn Bridge as the sun goes down. Another excellent place for a gorgeous view of the city and some very flattering pictures.

For dinner, if you were able to make a reservation for Lucali's, I'm low-key jealous of you. If not, worry not! You can go. to Keste Pizza, which is less crowded, has amazing pizza and pasta and great wine to go with it. I recommend everything about this restaurant except its decor, which is either vintage or just old.

Day 3 - morning

This happened to be the 4th of July for me, so plans were based mostly on whatever was open.

Start the day like a true New Yorker - with a bagel. Ess-a-bagel is the best place for this, but be warned that there'll be a line.

Next stop is the Top of the Rock. If you walk there, you'll be blessed with some pretty charming views of the city. While the most stunning view of the city will be at night, I recommend trying one of the high spots (Top of the Rock, the Edge, the Empire State Building) during the day just to get an idea of the layout of the city and be overwhelmed by it. Top of the Rock is a good choice for it.

If you have some time to spend here, you can also go shopping at the Rockefeller plaza.

Day 3 - afternoon

Again, wear your most comfy shoes because you're going to walk through a plenty galleries today.

You can go to the Metropolitan Museum of Art next. This might be an unpopular opinion, but if you have limited time and have been to plenty of museums before, you can skip this. Call me uncultured, but I would rather have absorbed more of the city than visited the MET (which might have something to do with the reservation here that fell through).

If you do go to the museum, here's what you should not sleep on - Greek and Roman Sculpture Court (Gallery 162), Van Gogh and Monet paintings (Gallery 826), The Temple of Dendur (Gallery 131), The American Wing Court (Gallery 700)

You'll also enjoy the vibe outside of the museum, with all the food stalls and street performances.

I lunched at the Shake Shack - which I do not recommend. Might just be my experience, but they had the worst fries I ever had. Don't miss out on the Levain Bakery though and try their Chocolate Chip Walnut cookies.

Last, you can visit the American Museum of Natural History, which is not too far and was where Night at the Museum was shot.

Day 3 - evening

As I mentioned before, Day 3, for us was 4th of July. So, the evening involved rushing to the 42nd, then 46th, and then 48th st trying to find a spot to look at the Macy's fireworks. If you're in New York on the 4th of July and you're not watching the fireworks, you're wasting your trip, no joke. As an international student, I have never felt this patriotic toward the US.

If you have some extra cash to spend, you can also go to any of the numerous cruizes and view the fireworks from the East River.

Day 4 - Morning

Central Park

Missing out on Central Park is definitely a bad idea on your trip to NYC. Central park is a serene place of lush greenery transporting you out of the concrete jungle situated right in the heart of Manhattan. Start your morning there possibly with a food takeout (central park is huge, you might get hungry). In this visit to Central Park, visit the following locations:

  • Belvedere castle
  • Bethesda Fountain and Terrace
  • Strawberry Fields (a memorial to John Lennon)

Beautiful New York neighborhoods

After a nice relaxing morning at Central Park, head out towards the scenic/foodie neighborhoods of NYC. Some options are Little Italy and Chinatown, but in our trip we headed straight to Washington Square Park to get a bite of the best NY dosas but were disappointed with the long queue with ETA of 2 hours. In the afternoon, roam around the lively neighborhood of Greenwich village starting from Washington Square Park. Grab a falafel at Mamoun's (best falafel in NYC) and a Cronut at Dominque Ansel Bakery on our foot tour.

Chelsea Market and the High Line park

Take a subway to Chelsea Market - a beautiful indoor shopping + food destination situation right in front of Google's NYC office. Avoid overpriced foods and skip unnecessary lines for food joints which you can get elsewhere (like Los Tacos No. 1).

A short walk from Chelsea market is the High Line Park - a 1.45 mile park built on an old metro line passing right thorough the city. Enjoy a nice walk and great views of the city. Expect the park to be crowded as the walking area is quite narrow.

Take some rest and food at your hotel before heading out to the speakeasy. We ordered in some tasty Indian Food from Dhaba Restaurant (their Vada Pav's were amazing!).

Drinks at a speakeasy

speakeasy: (during Prohibition) an illicit liquor store or nightclub.

Speakeasies are hidden/secret locations which serve drinks and unique cocktails with beautiful interiors. A good option is to head out to Garret which has a young vibe and is a small but cozy location serving a variety of amazing cocktails. Note that in our visit there were no food options here, so eating beforehand is recommend.

Empire State Building

Make a late night reservations to the Empire state building, in our case we made the reservation for 11 PM. After seeing NYC from the top of the rock, it cannot be stressed enough that you choose the second observation spot visit to be at night. The city looks completely different and the view at night will leave you in awe. Also, you can expect fewer visitors which can help you in taking your pictures unhindered. The cool breeze at night on the 86th floor of Empire State Building with the view as shown below will be a perfect way to cap off your day.

(Optionally) Head out to Times Square and return to hotel.

Day 5 - Morning

If you ever find yourself with some extra time in New York (how?!), go to Central Park! I went to the zoo, which is like most other zoos, but in Central Park! Top attractions will be the Red Panda (what a qt!), snow leopard, the Grizzley Bear, and the aviary. You can also visit the petting zoo here (which is in the Tisch Children's Zoo), but they don't have any dogs if that's why you're going (smh in self-disappointment).
